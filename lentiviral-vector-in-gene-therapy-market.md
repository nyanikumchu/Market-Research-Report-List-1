<p><p>Lentiviral vectors are a type of gene delivery system used in gene therapy, enabling stable gene expression in dividing and non-dividing cells. The market includes various viral vectors: Retrovirus (RV) vectors primarily target dividing cells but have limitations in gene size; Adenovirus (AdV) vectors can infect both dividing and non-dividing cells, though they typically induce strong immune responses; Adeno-associated Virus (AAV) vectors are favored for their safety, low immunogenicity, and ability to transduce a variety of cell types.</p></p>
