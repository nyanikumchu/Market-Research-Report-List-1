<p><p>Bipolar Junction Transistors (BJTs) are categorized into two main types: NPN and PNP. NPN transistors, which feature a layer of p-type material sandwiched between two n-type layers, are commonly used for amplification and switching applications due to their efficient electron mobility. Conversely, PNP transistors consist of a layer of n-type material between two p-type layers, functioning similarly but with holes as the majority charge carriers. Both types are critical in electronics, influencing demand in various sectors including consumer electronics and industrial applications.</p></p>
