<p><p>The smart card and security chip market can be divided into two primary types: contact and contactless. </p><p>Contact smart cards require physical insertion into a reader for data exchange, typically used in traditional payment systems and access control. In contrast, contactless smart cards utilize near-field communication (NFC) technology, enabling data transfer without physical contact, making them popular for mobile payments and public transportation. Both types enhance secure transactions, identity verification, and authentication across various industries, including finance, healthcare, and retail.</p></p>
