<p><p>Calcium chloride (CaCl2) is a versatile compound used in various industries. The market is primarily divided into two types: Calcium Chloride Dihydrate and Calcium Chloride Anhydrous. Dihydrate, with two water molecules, is commonly used for de-icing, dust control, and food preservation, while anhydrous, lacking water, is preferred in chemical processes and as a drying agent. Both types play significant roles in construction, agriculture, and refrigeration sectors, catering to diverse industrial needs based on their unique properties.</p></p>
